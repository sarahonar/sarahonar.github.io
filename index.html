About Me

Hi there! I'm Sara Honarvar, a PhD student at the University of Maryland, College Park. I specialize in Controls & Robotics, with a research focus on multi-agent systems, human interaction modeling, and machine learning. I love solving problems that merge robotics, AI, and control theory to create intelligent systems.

Education

  • Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering, University of Maryland, College Park (Expected 2025), GPA: 3.96/4

    At the University of Maryland, College Park, I am part of the Collaborative Controls and Robotics Lab, where I delve into advanced topics like inverse optimal control, multi-agent consensus, and autonomous systems. The vibrant research community and state-of-the-art facilities have been instrumental in shaping my academic journey.

  • M.A. in Kinesiology, University of Maryland, College Park (2019), GPA: 3.88/4

    My master's research in Neuromechanics explored the intersection of human motor control and robotics, providing me with a unique perspective on designing bio-inspired control systems.

  • B.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering, Amirkabir University of Technology, Tehran (2015), GPA: 3.83/4

    During my undergraduate studies, I specialized in Biomechanics, working on projects that combined engineering principles with medical applications to improve human health and well-being.
